How to Play Family Feud

How to Play Normal Rounds:

  1. Choose one person to be the "host." The host is the only player who can see the questions and answers.

  2. Split the rest of the players into 2 teams. For each question, one player from each team will be chosen to hear the question first. Those players will have one chance to answer the question. The team of the player whose answer was worth the most points gets to start the round.

  3. Players from that team guess one at a time, in order. Whenever a given answer overlaps with a previous answer, the host will tell the player to guess again. If an answer is too vague, the host will tell the player to be more specific. An incorrect answer earns the team a strike. If a team guesses all the answers before earning 3 strikes, the total value of their answers is added to their score.

  4. After 3 strikes, the other team has one chance to guess a remaining answer. If they guess correctly, they get to steal all of the points of the correct answers given by the other team.

How to Play Fast Money

  1. After the first 3 rounds, the winning team chooses 2 players to leave the room.

  2. One player comes back in and is given the 5 Fast Money questions. They only have one chance to answer each question. Add up the total for the answers that were correct.

  3. Then the second player returns and answers the questions in the same way. If the second player duplicates an answer, they must guess again. Add up this person’s total. Expect some funny answers.

  4. If they both combine to get to 200 points, the team is declared the ultimate winner!

  5. This round is usually timed (60 seconds), and really puts people on the spot!

Other Ways to Play

  • No teams: A free-for-all, where everyone tries to guess. This can get loud with more than 5 or so people. Scoring is optional. Everybody has more fun when all the answers are guessed, so when we play this, the host often ends up giving hints until all the answers are revealed.
  • Pen and Paper: This is popular at my house. Read all the questions in a row. Each player takes one guess for each question, writing their answers on a piece of paper or in their phone. Then you can see who got the highest score.
  • Pen and Paper, Fast Money only: This is how we play when we have less than 5 people, since someone has to be the host. After playing no teams for the first 3 questions in each round, we play Pen and Paper for the Fast Money questions. If more than one player makes the same guess, we only award points to one of them, and we try to break 200 total, just like the show.
  • Literal Words: The host only accepts answers exactly as they appear on the page. Another one where the host's discretion is advised.
  • Repeat Customers: Once you've done all the questions you can find, come back to them a week later. You'll be surprised just how many answers people don't remember. If you're playing with kids and these questions are not quite on their level, check out Family Feud Questions for Kids. If you're an adult looking for more questions, check out Family Feud Questions for Adults.
